Mr. Narendra Damodardas Modi

Narendra Damodardas Modi (born 17 September 1950) is an Indian politician who has served as the prime minister of India since 2014.
Modi was the chief minister of Gujarat from 2001 to 2014 and is the member of parliament (MP) for Varanasi.
He is a member of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) and of the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS), a right-wing Hindu nationalist paramilitary volunteer organisation.
He is the longest-serving prime minister outside the Indian National Congress. Modi was born and raised in Vadnagar, Bombay State (present-day Gujarat), where he completed his secondary education.

Ram Mandir

The Ram Mandir in Ayodhya is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Rama, an avatar of Vishnu, and is built on the site believed to be his birthplace. The temple's construction and inauguration have been a culmination of a long-standing historical and legal dispute.

Historical
  • The Ram Mandir in Ayodhya is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Rama, an avatar of Vishnu, and is built on the site believed to be his birthplace. The temple's construction and inauguration have been a culmination of a long-standing historical and legal dispute.
  • Babri Masjid: In 1528, the Babri Masjid, a mosque, was constructed at the same site by Mir Baqi, a general of the Mughal Emperor Babur. This led to centuries of religious and political conflicts.
  • Dispute and Demolition: The site became a flashpoint for communal tensions. In 1949, idols of Rama and Sita were placed inside the mosque. The Babri Masjid was ultimately demolished by Hindu activists in 1992, leading to widespread riots.
  • Supreme Court Verdict: In 2019, the Supreme Court of India delivered a historic verdict, awarding the disputed land to Hindus for the construction of a temple, while allocating land elsewhere for the construction of a mosque for Muslims.
Significance of Ram Mandir:-
  • Birthplace of Lord Rama: The primary significance of the Ram Mandir is its location at the revered spot believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama, a central figure in Hindu mythology and the epic Ramayana.
  • Symbol of Faith and Cultural Heritage: For millions of Hindus, the temple is not just a structure but a sacred ground, symbolizing their deep faith, devotion, and a living connection to Lord Rama. It also represents India's rich cultural identity and the enduring legacy of the Ramayana.
  • National Unity and Integration: For many, the construction of the temple is seen as a step towards healing historical wounds and fostering communal harmony, bringing a sense of national unity after decades of dispute.
  • Pilgrimage Site: The Ram Mandir is poised to become a major pilgrimage site, attracting devotees from across the globe and boosting tourism and the economy in Ayodhya.
