Some things about Vrindavan

Vrindavan, also spelled Vrindaban, is a historic town located in the Mathura district of Uttar Pradesh, India. It holds immense religious significance for Hindus, particularly for followers of Vaishnavism, as it is believed to be the place where Lord Krishna spent his childhood . The name "Vrindavan" is said to be derived from "Vrinda" (holy basil) and "vana" (grove or forest), referring to the dense forests and groves where Krishna performed his divine pastimes with Radha and the Gopis. Vrindavan is often referred to as the "City of Temples" due to its thousands of temples, many dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha.

Some of the prominent temples include:-

  • Banke Bihari Temple : Famous for its unique deity of Lord Krishna, where the curtain in front of the idol is opened and closed frequently.
  • Prem Mandir : A magnificent white marble temple known for its intricate carvings and beautiful illuminations, especially in the evening.
  • ISKCON Krishna Balaram Temple : A well-known temple built by the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, dedicated to Krishna and Balaram.
  • Radha Raman Temple : Houses a self-manifested deity of Krishna as Radha Ramana.
  • Nidhivan : A sacred grove believed to be the place where Krishna and Radha perform their divine dance (Ras Leela) every night. It is forbidden to stay inside after dusk.
  • Keshi Ghat : A significant ghat on the Yamuna River, associated with Krishna's pastimes.
Vrindavan is a major pilgrimage destination , attracting millions of devotees from all over the world who come to experience the spiritual atmosphere and connect with the divine stories of Krishna . The town comes alive during festivals like Janmashtami (Krishna's birthday) and Holi , with vibrant celebrations and devotional singing. It's a place where every corner resonates with the tales of Radha and Krishna's love and playful activities.
